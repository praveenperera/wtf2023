# Fallacy: it is just art

Source: https://wtfhappenedinfeb2023.com/common-narratives-around-spam/it-is-just-art

## It is just art

The issue is not whether art can exist. The issue is whether speculative data markets should externalize permanent storage and validation costs onto every Bitcoin node.

Calling arbitrary data "art" does not change the resource claim. When the data is committed to Bitcoin, it competes for blockspace and becomes part of what archival nodes must store. When the scheme creates spendable low-value outputs, it can also add pressure to the UTXO set every validating node must track.

Renaud Cuny reported 76 GB of non-financial data added over 3.5 years in Bitcoin Block Space Weekly Issue #3, published 2025-12-22. Mempool Research reported that inscription-related UTXOs accounted for 29.6% of the UTXO set count at block 892385 in its UTXO Set Report, published 2025-05-18.

Those sources support the storage and UTXO impact. Claims about stolen art, pump-and-dump activity, or organized market manipulation need their own sources and should not be bundled into this argument without receipts.
